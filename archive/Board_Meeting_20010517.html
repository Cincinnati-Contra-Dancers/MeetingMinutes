II. Annuciation Hall and Father Klug,   contact persons are Jim and Susan Vogt

                                                Look for a new hall to rent

                                                Look into building our own hall.

·         Financial sources for this:  Mary Lieneck said there may be funds, such as a grant funding available from the Fine Arts Fund for a hall.

V.  Community

A.      Long discussion regarding the meaning of community, what it means to the Cinti Contra Dancers

       and what it means individually.  Many comments and questions were raised, such as:

·         There are people who only come to dance.  (Debbie Barrett)

·         What else is being asked of them?  (Ron Hoffman)

·         There are those that don't want any more than to pay their money to dance for the evening.

·         Community to what you bring to it.  (Diane Silver)

·         If members are not feeling connected, have them create more events

·         Include new folks in invitations to UNO's or potlucks. (Barb Gomes)

·         Some people do feel included, asked to help and to come to this meeting. (Joe Lipari)

·         Organization – official events........Community – unofficial socialization

     B.  Announcements from the stage can be a way to include people in the community. 

                Several suggestions:

·         Have a single announcer, such as the caller to make all announcements.  This would, hopefully, decrease the amount of confusion of all the announcements.  In addition, the microphone is set for the caller and others are not as clearly understood.  The announcements could all the written down on a piece of paper by whomever needed to make an announcement and could be read at the beginning of the second set.

·         Each person make their own announcements.  This will show each person who is involved and demonstrate our high level of volunteerism in the group.  This suggestion seemed to be more favorable to the members attending the meeting.

C.        Schedule

·         Blow up the schedule and post on the bulletin board in the back, along with the descriptions

John requests a discussion on how to remove people from the database.  There is no indication of when people no longer want to be included in the mailings and on the ContraDance Phone List.  There are now over 900 people in the database.  There ensued a discussion on how and when to reliably remove names from the list.  Suggestions include:
